            <h1 class="text-4xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-6">Cooking with Cannabis: Delicious Infused Foods & Recipes</h1>
            <p class="text-gray-600 text-sm mb-6">Published on June 15, 2025 by Kingkush Team</p>
            
            <img src="https://placehold.co/800x400/059669/ffffff?text=Cannabis+Cooking" alt="Cooking with Cannabis" class="w-full rounded-lg mb-8 shadow-md">

            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Infusing cannabis into your culinary creations opens up a world of delightful possibilities, offering a discreet and often more potent alternative to traditional consumption methods. From decadent brownies to savory sauces, edibles provide a unique way to experience the plant's effects. However, successful cannabis cooking requires understanding a few key principles, primarily decarboxylation and accurate dosing.
            </p>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">Decarboxylation: The Essential First Step</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Raw cannabis contains cannabinoids in their acidic form (e.g., THCA, CBDA). To activate their psychoactive (THC) or therapeutic (CBD) properties, they must undergo decarboxylation – a process of heating the plant material. This removes a carboxyl group, converting THCA to THC and CBDA to CBD.
                <br><br>
                <strong>Simple Decarboxylation Method:</strong>
                <ol class="list-decimal list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-4 pl-4">
                    <li class="mb-2">Preheat your oven to 240°F (115°C).</li>
                    <li class="mb-2">Break up your cannabis flower into small, even pieces (do not grind too finely).</li>
                    <li class="mb-2">Spread the pieces evenly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.</li>
                    <li>Bake for 30-40 minutes, stirring gently halfway through. It should be lightly toasted and fragrant.</li>
                </ol>
            </p>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">Infusion Basics: Cannabutter and Cannabis Oil</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Once decarboxylated, the cannabinoids need to be extracted into a fat, as they are fat-soluble. Butter and oil are ideal choices because they absorb cannabinoids efficiently, making them versatile bases for almost any recipe.
            </p>
            <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-2">Cannabutter Recipe:</h3>
            <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 pl-4 leading-relaxed">
                <li class="mb-2"><strong>Ingredients:</strong> 1 cup unsalted butter, 7-14g decarboxylated cannabis (adjust for potency).</li>
                <li class="mb-2"><strong>Instructions:</strong>
                    <ol class="list-decimal list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-2 pl-4">
                        <li class="mb-1">Melt butter and 1 cup of water in a saucepan over low heat.</li>
                        <li class="mb-1">Add decarboxylated cannabis. Simmer on low for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally. Do not boil.</li>
                        <li class="mb-1">Strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a container. Squeeze out all liquid.</li>
                        <li>Refrigerate until the butter separates and solidifies on top. Discard the water.</li>
                    </ol>
                </li>
            </ul>
            <h3 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-2">Cannabis Oil Recipe (Coconut Oil is Popular):</h3>
            <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 pl-4 leading-relaxed">
                <li class="mb-2"><strong>Ingredients:</strong> 1 cup coconut oil, 7-14g decarboxylated cannabis.</li>
                <li class="mb-2"><strong>Instructions:</strong>
                    <ol class="list-decimal list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-2 pl-4">
                        <li class="mb-1">Combine oil and decarboxylated cannabis in a slow cooker or double boiler.</li>
                        <li class="mb-1">Heat on low for 4-6 hours, stirring occasionally. Keep temperature below 200°F (93°C).</li>
                        <li class="mb-1">Strain through cheesecloth into an airtight container.</li>
                        <li>Store in a cool, dark place.</li>
                    </ol>
                </li>
            </ul>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">Dosing and Consumption Tips</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Edibles can have a delayed and prolonged effect compared to smoking or vaping. Start with a low dose (e.g., 2.5-5mg THC) and wait at least 1-2 hours before consuming more. Always label your infused foods clearly and keep them out of reach of children and pets.
            </p>

            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                With careful preparation and mindful dosing, cooking with cannabis can be a rewarding and delicious way to enjoy its benefits. Experiment with different recipes, from cannabis-infused brownies and cookies to savory dishes like pasta sauces or salad dressings, to find your favorites.
            </p>
