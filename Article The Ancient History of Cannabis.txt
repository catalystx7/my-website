        <div class="max-w-4xl mx-auto px-4 sm:px-6 lg:px-8 bg-white shadow-lg rounded-lg p-8">
            <h1 class="text-4xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-6">The Ancient History of Cannabis: A Journey Through Time</h1>
            <p class="text-gray-600 text-sm mb-6">Published on June 15, 2025 by Kingkush Team</p>
            
            <img src="https://placehold.co/800x400/1a5f1a/ffffff?text=Ancient+Cannabis+History" alt="Ancient Cannabis History" class="w-full rounded-lg mb-8 shadow-md">

            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Cannabis, a plant shrouded in mystique and controversy in modern times, boasts a history that spans millennia, deeply intertwined with human civilization. Its journey began not as a recreational drug, but as a versatile resource indispensable for survival, medicine, and spiritual practices across ancient cultures.
            </p>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">Earliest Evidence: Asia's Cradle of Cannabis</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Archaeological evidence points to Central Asia as the birthplace of cannabis, with its earliest recorded uses dating back over 10,000 years. One of the most significant early discoveries comes from the Yangshao culture in China, around 4000 BCE, where hemp fibers were used to make pottery, textiles, and paper. The ancient Chinese pharmacopoeia, particularly the Shen Nung Pen Ts'ao Ching (believed to be compiled around 2737 BCE), details cannabis as a medicinal herb for various ailments, including pain, gout, and even absent-mindedness.
            </p>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">From Rituals to Remedies: India and the Middle East</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                The plant's migration led it to India, where it became deeply embedded in religious and spiritual practices. In the Atharva Veda, one of Hinduism's sacred texts (circa 1500 BCE), cannabis is lauded as one of the five sacred plants, believed to be a source of happiness, joy, and a liberator. It was consumed as "bhang" (a traditional edible preparation) and used in spiritual rituals to achieve altered states of consciousness and medicinal purposes.
                <br><br>
                Further west, ancient Persian texts, including the Zend-Avesta, mention *bhang* as a sacred herb, while the Assyrians used cannabis as an aromatic incense and a remedy for various illnesses, dating back to the 9th century BCE.
            </p>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">Spreading West: Europe and Africa</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                The Scythians, an ancient nomadic warrior people of Central Asia, are credited with introducing cannabis to Eastern Europe around the 7th century BCE. Herodotus, the Greek historian, famously documented their practice of inhaling cannabis smoke in ritualistic steam baths for purification and recreation.
                <br><br>
                In Africa, evidence suggests cannabis was used for medicinal and ritualistic purposes as early as the 1st century CE. Pipes containing cannabis residues have been found in Ethiopian caves, indicating early use across the continent.
            </p>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">Industrial Uses: Hemp's Enduring Legacy</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Beyond its psychoactive and medicinal properties, hemp (the non-intoxicating variety of cannabis) was revered for its robust fibers. Ancient civilizations relied on hemp for making ropes, sails, paper, clothing, and even building materials. This industrial utility ensured its widespread cultivation and transport across trade routes, further solidifying its presence in diverse geographical regions.
            </p>

            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                The ancient history of cannabis reveals a plant that was fundamental to human development, valued for its multifaceted applications long before modern prohibitions reshaped its perception. Understanding this rich past is crucial for appreciating its potential future.
            </p>
