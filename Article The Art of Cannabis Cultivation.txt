            <h1 class="text-4xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-6">The Art of Cannabis Cultivation: Planting & Growing Guide</h1>
            <p class="text-gray-600 text-sm mb-6">Published on June 15, 2025 by Kingkush Team</p>
            
            <img src="https://placehold.co/800x400/065f46/ffffff?text=Cannabis+Cultivation" alt="Cannabis Cultivation" class="w-full rounded-lg mb-8 shadow-md">

            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Growing your own cannabis can be a deeply rewarding experience, offering control over the quality, strain, and cultivation methods. Whether you're a novice or looking to refine your skills, understanding the fundamental stages and requirements of cannabis cultivation is key to a bountiful harvest. This guide provides an overview of the essential steps for planting and nurturing your plants.
            </p>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">1. Germination: Bringing Life to Your Seeds</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                The journey begins with germination. Cannabis seeds need warmth, moisture, and darkness to sprout.
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 pl-4 leading-relaxed">
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Paper Towel Method:</strong> Place seeds between two damp (not soaking wet) paper towels, put them in a Ziploc bag, and store in a warm, dark place (70-85째F or 21-29째C). Check daily for a taproot to emerge (usually 2-7 days).
                </li>
                <li>
                    <strong>Direct Sowing:</strong> Plant seeds directly into a small pot with moist starting mix. This minimizes transplant shock.
                </li>
            </ul>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">2. Seedling Stage: Delicate Beginnings</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Once the taproot appears, carefully transplant the seedling (if using the paper towel method) into a small pot with a light, well-draining soil mix. This stage is crucial for developing a strong root system.
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 pl-4 leading-relaxed">
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Lighting:</strong> Provide gentle light (e.g., fluorescent or low-wattage LED) for 18-24 hours a day. Avoid intense light which can stress young plants.
                </li>
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Watering:</strong> Water sparingly around the base of the plant, allowing the topsoil to dry out slightly between waterings. Overwatering is a common mistake.
                </li>
                <li>
                    <strong>Humidity:</strong> High humidity (60-70%) is beneficial during this stage.
                </li>
            </ul>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">3. Vegetative Stage: Growth Explosion</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                This is when your plant focuses on growing leaves, stems, and roots. You'll need to re-pot to a larger container.
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 pl-4 leading-relaxed">
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Lighting:</strong> Maintain 18-24 hours of light daily. Stronger lights (LED, HPS, MH) are needed now.
                </li>
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Nutrients:</strong> Introduce vegetative-specific nutrients, higher in nitrogen, as the plant needs these for leafy growth. Start with half doses.
                </li>
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Training (Optional):</strong> Techniques like topping, LST (Low Stress Training), or FIMing can be used to promote bushier growth and increase yields.
                </li>
                <li>
                    <strong>Environment:</strong> Maintain temperatures between 68-78째F (20-25째C) and humidity around 40-70%. Good airflow is crucial.
                </li>
            </ul>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">4. Flowering Stage: Bud Development</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                For photoperiod strains, switching to a 12 hours on/12 hours off light cycle triggers flowering. Auto-flowering strains will flower automatically.
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 pl-4 leading-relaxed">
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Lighting:</strong> Crucial 12/12 light cycle for photoperiod strains. Intense light is preferred for bud development.
                </li>
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Nutrients:</strong> Switch to flowering-specific nutrients, higher in phosphorus and potassium. Gradually reduce nitrogen.
                </li>
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Humidity:</strong> Lower humidity (40-50%) is ideal to prevent mold and mildew in developing buds.
                </li>
                <li>
                    <strong>Patience:</strong> This stage can last 8-11 weeks, depending on the strain.
                </li>
            </ul>

            <h2 class="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 mb-4">5. Harvesting, Drying, and Curing</h2>
            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Harvesting at the right time is critical for potency and flavor. Look for cloudy trichomes (tiny resin glands) with some amber.
            </p>
            <ul class="list-disc list-inside text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 pl-4 leading-relaxed">
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Harvest:</strong> Cut branches or the whole plant. Remove large fan leaves.
                </li>
                <li class="mb-2">
                    <strong>Drying:</strong> Hang branches in a dark room with good airflow (60-70째F and 50-60% humidity) for 7-14 days until stems snap.
                </li>
                <li>
                    <strong>Curing:</strong> Place dried buds in airtight glass jars, opening them daily ("burping") for the first week, then less frequently. This enhances flavor and smoothness, and prevents mold. Cure for at least 2-4 weeks, or longer.
                </li>
            </ul>

            <p class="text-lg text-gray-800 mb-6 leading-relaxed">
                Cultivating cannabis is an art and a science, requiring dedication and observation. With these foundational tips, you're well on your way to growing your own premium quality Kingkush plants. Always research specific strain needs and local regulations.
            </p>
